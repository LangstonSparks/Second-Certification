Some Facts about LeBron

  • LeBron James was born on December 30, 1984, in Akron, Ohio.
  • He attended St. Vincent-St. Mary High School in Akron, where he was a basketball prodigy, leading his team to three state championships in four years.
  • LeBron was the first overall pick in the 2003 NBA Draft, selected by the Cleveland Cavaliers straight out of highschool.
  • He won the NBA Rookie of the Year Award in 2004.
  • LeBron has won four NBA championshipsâ€”two with the Miami Heat (2012, 2013), one with the Cleveland Cavaliers (2016), and one with the Los Angeles Lakers (2020).
  • He has won the NBA Most Valuable Player (MVP) Award four times (2009, 2010, 2012, 2013).
  • LeBron has been selected to the NBA All-Star Game 19 times.
  • He has won two Olympic gold medals with Team USA, in 2008 (Beijing) and 2012 (London).
  • LeBron ranks among the top scorers in NBA history, surpassing Kareem Abdul-Jabbar to become the all-time leading scorer in February 2023.
  • He established the LeBron James Family Foundation, which supports various initiatives, including the I PROMISE School in Akron, aimed at providing education and resources to at-risk children.
